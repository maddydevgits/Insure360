Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Premium Money: 2% for kharif crops and 1.5% for rabi crops.

Eligibility Schemes: All farmers, including loanee and non-loanee farmers, cultivating notified crops in notified areas are eligible.

Benefits: PMFBY helps farmers manage the risks associated with farming, such as crop failure due to natural disasters or pests.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-Kisan)

Premium Money: ₹6000 per year in the bank account.

Eligibility Schemes: Small and marginal farmers with landholding up to 2 hectares.

Benefits: All qualified beneficiaries will get Rs 2000 quarterly amount.

National Agriculture Market (eNAM)

Premium Money: Minimum investment of ₹50 lakh.

Eligibility Schemes: It offers a “plug-in” to any market yard existing in a State (whether regulated or private).

Benefits: Facilitates better prices for a seller through transparent bidding, increased number of buyers from different markets, hence greater negotiation power.

Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme

Premium Money: Rs. 15 for a single-year policy.

Eligibility Schemes: You must be 18 years or older and under 75 years of age to apply for a KCC.

Benefits: Covered against financial losses of up to Rs. 50,000 against death, permanent total disability, and loss of limbs or eyes.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)

Premium Money: Rs. 50,000 crores over a five-year period.

Eligibility Schemes: Small and marginal farmers are eligible for PMKSNY.

Benefits: Developing water sources for guaranteed irrigation.

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

Premium Money: The ratio of 60:40 by the Central and State Governments respectively.

Eligibility Schemes: Aims at supporting and promoting organic farming, resulting in the improvement of soil health.

Benefits: Provides school/college dropouts or unemployed youth holding a valid ID (Aadhar/PAN) and bank account a chance to get skilled and employed.

Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)

Premium Money: 60:40 between Centre and States (90:10 for North Eastern States and Himalayan States).

Eligibility Schemes: Under NMSA, financial assistance up to 50% of the cost, subject to a limit of RS. 5,000/- per hectare and Rs. 10,000/- per beneficiary, is provided for the promotion of organic inputs.

Benefits: Provides funding up to ₹5 lakh (which is a 90% grant and 10% contribution to the incubate).

National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA)

Premium Money: Under the allocation of PM-AASHA ₹28,107 crore till 2025-26.

Eligibility Schemes: Agriculture with a special emphasis on soil & water conservation, water use efficiency, soil health management, and rainfed area development.

Benefits: Recently launched government scheme that guarantees remunerative prices to the farmers for their produce.

Pradhan Mantri Annadata Aay SanraksHan Abhiyan (PM-AASHA)

Premium Money: The ratio of 60:40 by the Central and State Governments respectively.

Eligibility Schemes: Agriculture with a special emphasis on soil & water conservation, water use efficiency, soil health management, and rainfed area development.

Benefits: Recently launched government scheme that guarantees remunerative prices to the farmers for their produce.
